VPPR Role Breakout

Dive into VPPR duties and learn to promote your club effectively

VPPR Role Breakout (0:40–1:10)

Dive into VPPR duties: promote the club, manage social media, create newsletters, and host events. Learn to use Canva, X, and track metrics like guest attendance.

Key Responsibilities

  • Post weekly on X with #Toastmasters.
  • Create flyers using Canva for events.
  • Track 1–2 guests per event as success.

Activity: PR Mini-Campaign

Draft a campaign idea (e.g., weekly X posts) and present it at the front. Audience suggests tweaks. Practice pitching to your club.

Officer Role Breakout Guide

Overview (5 minutes per group)

Facilitators use the Club Leadership Handbook to outline duties:

  • President: Sets tone, leads DCP planning, represents club at Area/District Councils.
  • VP Education: Schedules speeches, tracks Pathways, mentors members.
  • VP Membership: Recruits guests, follows up on applications.
  • VP Public Relations: Promotes club via social media, events.
  • Secretary: Keeps records, submits reports.
  • Treasurer: Manages dues, budgets (e.g., submit to World Headquarters by September 30).
  • Sergeant at Arms: Prepares meeting space, manages property.

Q&A and Tips (20 minutes)

  • Facilitator poses questions: "What's your biggest challenge?" "How can you support DCP goals?"
  • Example: VP Education group discusses scheduling new members first.
  • Share resources: Club Central for tracking, Leader Letter for updates.

Mentorship (5 minutes)

  • Pair new officers with experienced ones for quick advice (e.g., "How do I motivate shy members?").
  • Hand out role-specific checklists from the Club Leadership Handbook.

Materials

  • Club Leadership Handbook
  • Role checklists (PDF or printed), notepads, pens.
