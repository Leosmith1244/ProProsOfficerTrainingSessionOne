PR in the Real World

Hear from VPPRs about successful outreach and branding strategies

Spotlight: PR in the Real World (Panel Script) (0:25–0:40)

Moderator Introduction (1 minute)

Welcome to our PR Spotlight! Our Vice Presidents of Public Relations—PR Pro Member, DTM, PR Pro Member from a corporate club, and PR Pro Member from a community club—will share real-world outreach successes. Learn how to brand your club and attract guests, then ask your questions!

Panelist 1: PR Pros Member (2-3 minutes)

As District 106 PR Manager, I led a "Toastmasters Open House" campaign. We posted weekly on X and Facebook, using hashtags like #ToastmastersD106. One post—a video of a member's Ice Breaker speech—got 50 likes and brought 3 guests to our meeting. My tip: Share authentic member stories on social media. It's free and draws curious visitors. Track 1–2 guests per event as a win, per our District's focus on achievable metrics.

Panelist 2: PR Pros Member, Corporate Club (2-3 minutes)

In our corporate club, we struggled with visibility. I organized a "Lunch & Learn" at our company, inviting employees to watch a mini-speech contest. We used flyers in the break room and got 10 guests; 4 joined. My tip: Partner with HR to promote Toastmasters as professional development. Track sign-ups to measure success.

Panelist 3: PR Pros Member, Community Club (2-3 minutes)

Our community club hosted a "Speak Up Saturday" at a local library. We advertised via free community boards and got 15 attendees, with 2 new members. My tip: Use local venues for events and track attendance. Even one new member is a victory!

Q&A (3 minutes)

Moderator: "What's one PR challenge you face?" (Audience asks; panel responds briefly.)
Example: "How do I get more X followers?"
Leo: "Post consistently—once a week—and engage with comments. It builds slowly but surely."

Success Stories

  • meetup campaign with #ToastmastersD106 drew 3 guests.
  • Corporate lunch & learn brought 4 new members.
  • Library event attracted 5 attendees.

Activity: PR Pitch-Off

Pitch a club event (e.g., open house) at the front of the room. Use a tactic like social media or community outreach. Audience votes for the best pitch.
